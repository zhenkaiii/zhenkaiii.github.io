Scalable and Efficient Neural Speech Coding: a Hybrid Design

We propose a scalable and efficient neural speech codec. Through a hybrid design, both LPC and residual coding are integrated in network training.

List of strengths:

  • It's efficient
    • Our neural codec is based on a lightweight neural speech coding module (NSC) with only 0.35 million parameters.
    • The decoder of NSC contains only 0.12 million parameters which is 100x smaller than a WaveNet decoder.
    • It can run on a single core CPU without further hardware adapted optimization, such as network pruning and quantization. The processing (encoding and decoding) time is less than the utterance duration.
  • It's scalable
    • Unlike neural vocoders, our codec conducts waveform coding, supporting a wide range of bitrates from 12 to 32 kbps.
    • The speech coding performance is comparable or superior to AMR-WB and Opus, both operating at the same or higher bitrates.

List of limitations:

  • Although it is significantly smaller than other neural vocoders, its runtime complexity is still higher than traditional codecs such as AMR-WB. To meet the case of edge applications, the model weights may need to be quantized and pruned.
